# Chapter Map

## Part I — Mathematical Foundation

### Chapter 2: Manifolds

Introduces topological and differentiable manifolds, coordinate charts, differentiable structures, diffeomorphisms, and metrics.

### Chapter 3: Tensor Spaces

Develops tangent spaces, cotangent spaces, tensor fields, pushforward, pullback, and coordinate transformation rules.

### Chapter 4: Differential Forms

Introduces exterior algebra, differential forms, exterior derivative, integration, Hodge duality, and related operations.

### Chapter 5: Lie Derivatives, Lie Groups, and Lie Algebras

Reviews Lie groups, Lie algebras, Lie derivatives, group actions, structure equations, Cartan subalgebras, and related symmetry structures.

### Chapter 6: Fibre Bundle Foundation

Introduces fibre bundles, vector bundles, principal bundles, transition functions, sections, associated bundles, product bundles, Whitney sums, and tensor product bundles.

### Chapter 7: Connections on Principal Bundles

Develops connection one-forms, local connection forms, curvature two-forms, gauge transformations, Bianchi identities, and the geometric meaning of curvature.

### Chapter 8: Geometrical Meaning of Connection and Covariant Derivative

Discusses horizontal lifts, parallel transport, holonomy, covariant derivatives on associated bundles, and characteristic classes.

### Chapter 9: Clifford Algebra

Develops Clifford algebras, Clifford products, grade structure, spinor representations, chirality, spinor products, charge conjugation, Pin groups, Spin groups, and spacetime spin algebra.

## Part II — Physics Foundation

### Chapter 10: Abelian Gauge Theories

Formulates Maxwell theory in U(1) bundle language.

### Chapter 11: Non-Abelian Gauge Theories

Develops Yang-Mills theory, non-Abelian curvature, adjoint bundles, solitons, instantons, and coupling normalization.

### Chapter 12: Gravitation and Spin Bundle

Formulates gravity using orthonormal frames, spin bundles, spin connections, tetrads, curvature, torsion, and the Dirac operator on curved spacetime.

### Chapter 13: Anomalies

Reviews Abelian, non-Abelian, gravitational, and global anomalies.

## Part III — Standard Model and Beyond

### Chapter 14: Standard Model of Particle Physics

Reviews Standard Model gauge structure, representations, electroweak symmetry breaking, Yukawa terms, and anomalies.

### Chapter 15: SU(5) Georgi-Glashow Model

Studies SU(5) grand unification, Standard Model embedding, representation structure, symmetry breaking, and anomalies.

### Chapter 16: Spin(10) Model

Develops the Spin(10) grand-unified model, including Clifford and spin algebra, irreducible representations, symmetry breaking, charge operators, fermionic sectors, Higgs sectors, anomalies, and the complete curved-spacetime action.

### Chapter 17: Conclusion and Outlook

Summarizes the bundle-level Spin(10) framework and discusses future directions.
