#Mathematical Background

##Manifolds and Tensor Geometry

The thesis begins with smooth manifolds as the geometric setting for field theory. Tensor fields, differential forms, metrics, and coordinate transformations provide the local and global language needed to express physical laws in a coordinate-independent form.

##Differential Forms and Hodge Duality

Differential forms provide a compact language for integration, exterior differentiation, gauge curvature, and topological densities. The Hodge dual is used to formulate kinetic terms and field equations in a metric-dependent way.

##Lie Groups and Lie Algebras

Lie groups and Lie algebras describe continuous symmetries. In this thesis, they appear both in spacetime symmetry and internal gauge symmetry. The relevant groups include Lorentz-related spin groups, Standard Model gauge groups, SU(5), and Spin(10).

##Fibre Bundles

Fibre bundles provide the global geometric language for fields. Locally, a bundle may look like a product space, but globally it may contain non-trivial transition functions. This makes fibre bundles essential for gauge theory, gravity, monopoles, instantons, and topological sectors.

##Principal Bundles

Principal bundles encode gauge symmetry geometrically. Gauge fields are interpreted as connections on principal bundles, and gauge transformations arise from changes of local trivialization.

##Associated Vector Bundles

Matter fields are described as sections of associated vector bundles. Once a principal bundle and a representation of its structure group are specified, one can construct the associated bundle carrying matter fields.

##Connections and Curvature

A connection defines covariant differentiation and parallel transport. Its curvature measures the non-commutativity of covariant derivatives and is physically interpreted as field strength. This applies both to Yang-Mills gauge theory and to spin connections in gravity.

##Clifford Algebras

Clifford algebras refine the exterior algebra by incorporating a metric. They provide the algebraic foundation for gamma matrices, spinors, chirality, charge conjugation, and the construction of Pin and Spin groups.

##Spin Groups and Spinor Representations

Spin groups are double covers of orthogonal groups and provide the correct representation theory for fermions. Spin(1,3) describes Lorentz spinors, while Spin(10) provides a grand-unified representation structure capable of containing one Standard Model family in a chiral spinor representation.

##Characteristic Classes and Topology

Characteristic classes encode global topological information about bundles. They are relevant for anomalies, instantons, monopoles, and the global consistency of gauge and spin structures.
