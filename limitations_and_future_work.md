# Limitations and Future Work

## Limitations

This thesis is primarily structural, mathematical, and classical/semi-classical. It is not intended to provide a complete phenomenological analysis of Spin(10) grand unification.

Main limitations include:

- No quantitative proton-decay prediction
- No full threshold correction analysis
- No full renormalization-group unification study
- No collider-phenomenology analysis
- No complete treatment of supersymmetric extensions
- No full quantum-gravity dynamics
- No numerical phenomenological fit to fermion masses
- No complete scan of Spin(10) Higgs potentials
- No experimental model selection between competing GUT breaking chains

## Future Work

Possible future directions include:

- Study Spin(10) symmetry-breaking chains more systematically
- Analyze renormalization-group running and coupling unification
- Study proton-decay constraints
- Develop phenomenological predictions for neutrino masses
- Compare minimal and non-minimal Higgs sectors
- Study effective field theory matching below the GUT scale
- Explore anomaly constraints in greater detail
- Connect the bundle framework with modern scattering-amplitude methods
- Study topological sectors and characteristic classes more systematically
- Compare the Spin(10) bundle formulation with other geometric approaches to unification

## Research Outlook

The thesis provides a mathematical and geometric framework. A natural next step is to connect this framework with phenomenology, effective field theory, renormalization-group analysis, and modern formal methods in high-energy theory.
