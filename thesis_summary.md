#Thesis Summary

##Motivation

The Standard Model successfully describes known gauge interactions, but its gauge group is factorized into separate components and its matter representations are distributed across different group factors. This motivates the search for a more economical high-energy structure.

At the same time, spinor fields arise naturally from the local Lorentz structure of spacetime and are described using Clifford algebras and spin groups. Coupling spinors to curved spacetime requires the language of tetrads, spin connections, and spin bundles.

This thesis studies grand unification from the viewpoint of curved spacetime and asks how Spin(10) gauge fields and Lorentz spinors can be formulated together in a globally defined, coordinate-free geometric framework.

##Core Question

How can Clifford algebras, spin groups, principal bundles, associated spinor bundles, covariant derivatives, and curvature be used to formulate Spin(10) grand unification on curved spacetime?

##Conceptual Strategy

The thesis keeps two kinds of structure conceptually distinct:

1. Spacetime spin geometry, governed by Spin(1,3).
2. Internal grand-unified gauge symmetry, governed by Spin(10).

Rather than merging spacetime and internal symmetry into a single group, the thesis develops a common mathematical toolkit using Clifford algebras and principal bundles.

##Main Construction

The thesis works on a four-dimensional Lorentzian spacetime manifold admitting a spin structure. It constructs the Lorentz spin bundle and the Spin(10) grand-unified gauge bundle over the same spacetime base. Associated spinor bundles are then used to describe fermionic matter, and a unified covariant derivative is defined whose connection contains both the spin connection and the Spin(10) gauge connection.

##Main Topics

- Manifolds, tensors, differential forms, and Lie groups
- Fibre bundles, principal bundles, associated bundles
- Connections, curvature, parallel transport, and holonomy
- Clifford algebras, spinors, Pin groups, and Spin groups
- Abelian and non-Abelian gauge theory
- Gravity and spin bundles
- Anomalies
- The Standard Model
- SU(5) grand unification
- Spin(10) grand unification
- Curved-spacetime Spin(10) action

##Main Outcome

The thesis provides a self-contained bundle-level framework for classical analysis of Spin(10) grand unification on curved spacetime. It clarifies how Spin(10) matter can be coupled to gravity, how the relevant bundles and covariant derivatives are organized, and how Standard Model representations arise inside the grand-unified structure.
